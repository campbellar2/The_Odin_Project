Spaghetti and Meatballs

Homepage

Description

A classic Italian/Italian-American pasta dish, consisting of spaghetti pasta, tomato sauce, and meatballs. It was a popular dish among Italian immigrants in New York City in the late 1800's/early 1900's.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of spaghetti, thin spaghetti, or bucatini pasta
  • 1 pound of ground beef (16% fat or higher)
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 3/4 cups of unseasoned breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup of Parmesan-Romano cheese blend
  • 2 (28-ounce) cans of crushed tomatoes (San Marzano, if possible)
  • 3 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2-3 teaspoons of salt & pepper
  • 4 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • about 1/2 cup of red wine
  • 3 cloves of chopped garlic
  • 1/2 copped yellow onion
  • 1/4 cup of chopped basil
  • 1/4 cup of chopped parsley

Steps

  1. Prepare the Sauce:

    Heat half the olive oil in a large saucepan/pot on medium high heat. Add onions, cook for 2 minutes. Add garlic, cook until fragrant (about 30 seconds).

    Add tomato paste concentrate, stir to blend, and cook for 1 minute. Then add the canned tomatoes, and most of the basil and parsley, keeping some for the meatballs. Stir the sauce until it starts to thicken, about 5 minutes.

    Reduce heat to a simmer, stirring occasionaly while working on the next steps.

  2. Form the Meatballs:

    In a large bowl, mix the beef, pork, basil, breadcrumbs, half the cheese, and 2 teaspoons of salt and pepper. Don't over-mix, however, as that will make the meatballs too tough.

    Use a melon baller, or teaspoon, to form 1-inch round meatballs. Roll them and compress into tight balls.

  3. Brown the meatballs:

    In a separate wide, shallow pan, heat remaining olive oil on medium high heat. Brown the meatballs on all sides, about 2-3 minutes.

    You may need to do it in two batches, depending on the size of your pan. Don't overcrowd the pan, just cook them in a single layer. Don't overcook the meatballs either, they will continue to cook in the sauce. Just brown the sides.

  4. Finish and simmer the sauce:

    Add the wine to the sauce and stir in the remaining cheese. Add salt to taste. Add meatballs gently and stir slowly. Simmer the sauce and meatballs on low heat for 30 - 45 minutes, stirring occasionaly.

  5. Cook the pasta:

    While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot with 4 quarts of salted water in it to a boil (1 tablespoon of salt for every 2 quarts of water). When the water returns to a boil add the pasta to the pot.

    Leave the pot uncovered while you cook the pasta on high heat with a vigorous boil. Put a timer on for 8 to 10 minutes, or according to package directions for al dente pasta (cooked but still a little firm). Drain.

  6. Serve:

    To serve, place thin layer of sauce on plate, add pasta, add sauce and meatballs, and then sprinkle with grated Parmesan-Romano cheese.
